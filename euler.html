M.

power repeatedly applies the formula as long as the boolean condition on the RHS, 1&<, is true.

The double recursive Fibonacci definition \(F_{n} = F_{n-2} + F_{n-1}\) is encoded in the fork (-&2 +&$: -&1). The middle verb +&$: composes addition, which is applied in dyadic form, with f0b (as $:), which is applied in monadic form. The fork is literally \(F_{n-2} + F_{n-1}\).

In essays/Fibonacci Sequence, another formula is given: \(F_{n+k} = F_k F_{n+1} + F_{k-1} F_{n}\). Since every third element of the Fibonacci sequence is even, we can use \(F_{n+3} = 2 F_{n+1} + F_{n} = 3 F_{n} + 2 F_{n-1}\). However this still requires computing entire sequence. Let's just filter list to keep every third and therefore even term.
